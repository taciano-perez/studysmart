Making the grade

Target: keep your grades out of the red zone

  1. Study a minimum of five hours per week. One cannot learn without regular practice.
  2. When studying, turn off distractions. You can't learn to concentrate if you self-interrupt.
  3. Study with one of your parents at least once per week. They can help you to concentrate.
  4. Sleep at least eight hours at night when you have school on the next day. You can't learn if you're tired.
  5. Quit the phone one hour before sleep time when you have school on the next day.
  6. Attend all classes. You can't learn if you're not there.
  7. Deliver all school assignments, on time, and putting effort. You can't afford to miss opportunities to keep your grades good.
  8. Starting a week before and during test weeks, all effort goes to reviewing and studying for tests. No going out in this period.
  9. Record progress here daily. You cannot improve what you don't measure.
  10. If your grades are in danger of going red, ask for help. We can help you, or hire tutors.
